Chess Dojo

The Chess Dojo App is a mobile application designed to help chess players of all skill levels improve their game. The app offers a variety of features such as puzzles, lessons, and games that are designed to challenge and educate players on different aspects of chess strategy and tactics. The app also offers a community feature where players can connect with others to discuss strategies and participate in tournaments. With its user-friendly interface and comprehensive curriculum, the Chess Dojo App is the perfect tool for anyone looking to take their chess skills to the next level.

Chess PGN Master

Chess PGN Master is a chess learning and study tool available for both amateur and professional players. The app allows players to review and analyze games, enter their own games, and analyze them with a powerful chess engine. The app supports several features such as E-Board, cloud support, Chess960, and autoplay. It also allows easy navigation and direct sharing of games. The trial version of the app allows users to view the first 20 games of every PGN file, but buying the pro key removes restrictions and enables saving changed games. While many users consider Chess PGN Master to be one of the best chess apps available on Android, some users have reported difficulties with the app.

Chess for Android

Chess for Android consists of a chess engine (a Java version derived from the C++ engine BikJump) and a GUI. The application accepts moves through the touch screen, trackball, or keyboard (e2e4 pushes the king pawn, e1g1 castles king side, etc.). An optional "move coach" highlights valid user moves during input and last played engine move. Full game navigation enables users to correct mistakes or analyze games. Games import and export as FEN/PGN to and from the clipboard or via sharing, load and save as file, or are set up through a position editor. A draw by stalemate, insufficient material, the fifty move rule, or threefold repetition is recognized. The engine plays at various levels (including random, against itself in auto-play, or free-play, where the game can be used as a "magnetic chessboard"). The user can play either side and, independently, view the board from the perspective of white or black.

Graham's Driver

Graham's chess driver is an impressive driver designed to enhance the chess-playing experience for enthusiasts of all levels. Created by Graham O'Neill, a software developer from New Zealand, it offers compatibility with other popular chess applications, such as Fritz, Arena, LucasChess, and Shredder. With its sleek design and powerful functionality, Graham's chess driver is a must-try for anyone looking to improve their chess skills or simply enjoy a fun game with friends.
